Nobel Prize Winners Analysis

The Nobel Prizes are awarded in the fields of Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, Peace and Economy. The price winners are awarded for their “contributions that have conferred the greatest benefit to humankind” based on Alfred Nobel, the founder of Nobel Prize.

This visualization aims to present the Nobel prize winners globally throughout 1901 to 2019 with comprehensive insights.

This ISOTYPE shows the Nobel prize winners in each category throughout 1901 to 2019. By using pictographs as the marks, users can easily identify the ratio of male and female changes throughout the years.

In this graph, females tend to perform better at peace and literature with 12.69% and 12.93% respectively. Bur poorly on physics with 1.41%. However, Marie Curie has awarded physics Nobel prize twice in 1903 and 1963 respectively and has also awarded chemistry Nobel prize in 1911. She has awarded the greatest number of Nobel prize in history

The Demographics of Nobel Prize Winner

The symbol chart map uses to show the demographics of Nobel prize winners around the world. Circle is uses as the marks to indicate the total number of Nobel laureates a country has/had. A stacked bar chart is also used below to help improving the interactivity. It allows user to select an interval and drag it along the x axis so any countries within the intervals will also be highlighted on the map.

Based on the graph, USA and European countries have very high number of laureates. The varieties of laureates are also diverse; While most developing countries have only one laureate, and the most common category is peace. Another interesting face is that Germany had the most laureates since 1901 to 1950. However, after the loss of WWII, USA surpassed Germany and kept in #1 position ever since.

Ratio of Female and Male Representativity

This donut chart shows the ratio differences between male and female laureates throughout 1901 to 2019.

Based on the idiom, female has an increasing ratio in peace, medicine, and literature. It is likely due to that more regions started to allow woman to get proper education. This is especially remarkable in peace field; many female from third world countries such as Iraq, Liberia, Pakistan awarded prices.

Age Distribution of Nobel Prize Winner

The whisker box plot shows the age distribution of laureates in terms of different field and gender. Each fields are distinguished between color hues; Filters allow users to explore the age distribution changes throughout the years.

Based on the idiom, the age distribution for literature Nobel prize winners went down during 1914 to 1950. This is the period when WWI and WWII took place, people were heavily affected by the war, and wanted to express their emotions through writings.
